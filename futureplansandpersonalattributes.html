Section A Long-Term Plans & Deadlines

📌 Grammar Notes: Future Perfect

We use the Future Perfect to talk about actions that will finish before a given time in the future. It is heavily used for deadlines and bucket lists.

  • Structure: Subject + will have + Verb (Past Participle)
  • Deadlines: "By Friday, I will have finished the FY26 report."
  • Bucket Lists: "Before I die, I will have traveled to Japan."

💡 Hint: Recuerda la estructura: "will have + participio pasado" (como en tu ejemplo: I will have gotten in shape...).

  2. 2. Before the end of Q3, I (write) the new training manual.
  3. 3. When she retires next year, she (work) here for two decades.
  4. 4. By 5:00 PM tomorrow, the team (send) the final proposal to the client.

Section B Personal Attributes in Job Interviews

📌 Grammar Notes: Advanced Adverbs & Idioms

Upgrade your vocabulary for job interviews! Instead of basic adverbs, use stronger words to describe your personality.

  • Very: pretty, highly, most, really
  • Fairly: rather, enough, indeed
  • Not very: least, less than you think, not too, not really, scarcely
  • Too / Extremely: utterly, intensely, strongly, fully, completely
  • Work Idioms: early bird (morning person), night owl (night worker), clever as a fox (smart), stubborn as a mule (inflexible).

💡 Hint: Revisa tu tabla de apuntes. "Utterly" es equivalente a "Too/Extremely". "Highly" es "Very". "Scarcely" es "Not very".
