📌 Grammar Notes: The Four Futures

  • Future Simple (Will): Spontaneous decisions, promises, or predictions without evidence.
    "I believe artificial intelligence will change everything."
  • Be + Going to: Planned intentions and predictions based on present evidence.
    "The company lost its biggest client. They are going to lay off employees."
  • Present Progressive (Be + -ing): Fixed arrangements, dates, or meetings with other people.
    "I am meeting the suppliers on Tuesday morning."
  • Future Progressive (Will be + -ing): Actions that will be in progress around a specific time.
    "During the flight, I will be reading the reports."
