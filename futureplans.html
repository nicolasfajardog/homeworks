📌 Grammar Notes: The Four Futures

  • Future Simple (Will): Spontaneous decisions, promises, or predictions without evidence.
    "Don't worry, I will help you with those bags."
  • Be + Going to: Planned decisions and predictions based on present evidence.
    "Look at the traffic! We are going to be late."
  • Present Progressive (Be + -ing): Fixed arrangements, dates, or meetings with other people.
    "I am meeting the investors tomorrow morning."
  • Future Progressive (Will be + -ing): Actions that will be in progress at a specific time in the future.
    "At 8 PM tonight, I will be having dinner."
