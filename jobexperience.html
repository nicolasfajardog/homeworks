📌 Grammar Notes: The Present Perfect

We use the present perfect for expressing actions and situations from the past that continue in the present.
Structure: HAVE / HAS + PARTICIPLE

  • Questions: We can use yet, already, before and ever.
    "Have you ever worked in marketing?"
    "Have you finished your master's yet?"
  • Affirmative: We can use already and before.
    "I have already finished my undergrad."
  • Negative: We can use never, yet and before.
    "I have never worked in marketing."
    "I haven't started it yet."

💡 Position rules: Ever, never, and already go before the participle. Yet and before go at the end of the sentence.
