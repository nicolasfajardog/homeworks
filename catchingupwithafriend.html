Grammar Life Updates

📌 Grammar Notes: Perfect Tenses

  • Present Perfect: (Have/has + participle) Used for experiences or finished actions that matter now.
    "I have started a new job."
  • Present Perfect Continuous: (Have/has + been + -ing) Used for actions that started in the past and are still ongoing.
    "I have been working at SLB."
  • For vs. Since:
    • For: Periods of time (duration). "For 10 months."
    • Since: The point of beginning. "Since September 2025."
  • Useful Words: already, yet, before, never, ever, recently, lately.
