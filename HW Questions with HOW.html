How often?

📌 Grammar Reference

We use How often? to ask about frequency.

Adverbs of frequency go before the main verb (but after "be"):

always (100%) usually (~80%) sometimes (~50%) rarely (~10%) never (0%)

✏️ She always drinks coffee. · I never eat fish.

Expressions of frequency go at the end of the sentence:

once a day twice a week three times a month every year weekly monthly quarterly

✏️ I go to the gym twice a week. · We have a meeting quarterly.

B

How much / How many?

📌 Grammar Reference

How many? → countable nouns (you can count them: 1 apple, 2 apples…)

How much? → uncountable nouns (you can't count them: water, rice, money…)

Use there is (singular/uncountable) and there are (plural countable).

Make uncountable nouns countable with expressions:

a bottle of water a box of cereal a cup of tea a glass of juice a bag of rice a slice of bread a piece of cake

Quantifiers:

a little (uncountable – small amount) a few (countable – small number) a lot of (both)

✏️ There is a little milk. · There are a few eggs. · There is a lot of rice.

C

How long does it take?

📌 Grammar Reference

We use How long does it take? to ask about travel time or the time needed for an activity.

Q: How long does it take to drive to Santa Marta?
A: It takes 10 hours to drive to Santa Marta.

Q: How long does it take to walk to school?
A: It takes 20 minutes to walk to school.

How long? · How tall? · How old?

📌 Grammar Reference

We use How + adjective? to ask about measurements. The answer ends with the same measuring word (the adjective).

Question Answer pattern Example
How old is he?He is ___ years old.He is 10 years old.
How tall is she?She is ___ cm / m tall.She is 165 cm tall.
How long is it?It is ___ metres long.It is 200 m long.
How heavy is it?It is ___ kg heavy.It is 5 kg heavy.
How deep is it?It is ___ metres deep.It is 100 m deep.
How wide is it?It is ___ metres wide.It is 3 m wide.
